Forbes x Female Financial Literacy

Closing the Financial Literacy Gap, Feb 2, 2020

Women today are more empowered in business, work, and society than before, but many don’t feel empowered about their financial future. Closing the financial literacy gap is essential because the future of finance is female. Women live longer and are projected to inherit 70% of future wealth over the next two generations, so they need to be financially literate to secure their future and manage many of the nation’s assets. On top of that, women deserve economic equality and representation, and financial literacy will help close the gender pay gap. Ensuring women are financially empowered is crucial for not only them, but for society as well.

Clever Girl Finance

A personal finance platform, Jan 23, 2020

Clever Girl Finance is a financial education platform that provides women with online courses, mentorship, and access to a community of women working towards financial independence. It focuses on helping women save money, escape debt, and build wealth through the help of their coveted roadmap to financial success tailored to give women the knowledge and confidence needed to handle their money in the best way. Bola Sokunbi, founder of Clever Girl Finance, does a great job in motivating women and reminding them that they, too, have what it takes to be financially accountable and successful.

Women and Financial Literacy

Make Informed Financial Decisions, Jan 5, 2020

Don't miss! The historic gender inequality continues to affect all women to this day. Despite our efforts to close the gap in the workforce and education, we forget one aspect that makes our efforts worth it: financial literacy!

Women are more likely to have significantly lower rates of financial literacy compared to men even though they make up the majority of caregivers and are 3 times more likely than men to quit their jobs to care for family. These obstacles required serious dedication to planning to be able to overcome them. Read More on how low financial literacy can make getting ahead harder for women and how to overcome these discrepancies to get one step closer to financial independence in the future.
